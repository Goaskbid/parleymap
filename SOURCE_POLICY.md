# Source Catalog

## Primary source types

- Official calendars and diaries for heads of state, heads of government, ministers, commissioners, royals, and institutional leaders.
- Public schedules and press guidance pages.
- International-organization calendars, speeches, press releases, and meeting records.
- Host-event agendas, speaker lists, summit pages, and communiques.
- Official social media posts from verified institutional or office accounts, subject to API/terms compliance.
- Official photo/media libraries where licensing is explicit.

## Secondary source types

- Wire services and reputable media reports.
- GDELT and other event/story discovery tools.
- Wikipedia/Wikidata for identity hints and public role metadata.
- OpenSanctions PEP datasets for role/entity discovery and entity resolution.

Secondary sources should help discover candidates. They should not be the final authority for sensitive or future location records unless editorial policy explicitly permits it and corroboration exists.

## Suggested connector groups

1. EU and European institutions.
2. US federal public schedules and department calendars.
3. UK Royal Diary and other royal household calendars.
4. UN system leadership pages, speech pages, and event calendars.
5. World Bank, IMF, OECD, WTO, WHO, GAVI, BIS, central-bank, and development-bank calendars.
6. G7/G20/COP/WEF/Munich/UNGA event host sites.
7. National government leader and foreign ministry pages.
8. Major company investor/newsroom/event pages.
9. Major foundations and philanthropy newsrooms.
10. Global sport bodies such as FIFA, IOC, UEFA, and host-event pages.

## Record-level source pack

Each appearance should store:

- URL.
- Source title.
- Source type.
- Publisher/organization.
- Retrieved timestamp.
- Publication timestamp where available.
- Extracted text snippet or structured field.
- License/terms note.
- Robots/usage note where relevant.
- Source reliability score.
- Hash of fetched content for audit.

## v2.0 source additions

The summit watch and peace-process layer should prioritize official public pages from host governments, multilateral bodies, official summit websites, royal households, corporate investor-relations pages, and event organizers.

Initial official/host source categories added in v2.0 include:

- G7 host pages for future summit cards.
- NATO pages and media advisories for summit watch cards.
- United Nations calendar pages for UNGA, COP, AI/governance, and multilateral public-event watch.
- U.S. State Department and official summit pages for G20 public-event watch.
- Swiss FDFA pages for Bürgenstock 2024 peace summit records.
- White House public video/transcript pages for U.S. official public-event records.

Do not use leaked itineraries, hotel sightings, flight data, paparazzi images, private-residence claims, or unofficial social media sightings as publication sources.
