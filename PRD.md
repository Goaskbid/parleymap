# ParleyMap V2.0 Product Expansion

## Core positioning

ParleyMap is not a travel tracker. It is a global influence intelligence platform.

The product helps users understand where power is moving, who is meeting whom, which relationships are strengthening, which cities are becoming strategic hubs, which industries may be affected, and which public events may have market relevance.

**Primary line:** Understand where influence is forming.

**Tagline:** Public appearances. Mapped intelligently.

## Product pillars

### 1. Encounter intelligence

Create `Encounter` as a first-class entity. An encounter can be a publicly documented meeting, summit, conference appearance, bilateral meeting, state visit, delegation visit, royal engagement, investor summit, roadshow, or strategic public event.

Each encounter should show participants, organizations represented, date, location, verification source, importance score, public documentation links, and a concise Why This Matters summary.

### 2. Relationship network

Each encounter creates a relationship edge. Edges should be scored by frequency, recency, strategic importance, event significance, verification level, and network role.

Network modes should include heads of state, royal network, sovereign wealth funds, technology leadership, corporate executives, defense/security, finance, energy, climate, health, and multilateral organizations.

### 3. Influence rankings

Dynamic rankings should cover global influence, political leaders, corporate leaders, royal figures, sovereign wealth leaders, diplomatic hubs, strategic cities, summit locations, and fastest-growing relationships.

Every ranking must be explainable. Users should be able to inspect the score components.

### 4. Geopolitical heatmap

The map must answer: Where is power concentrating?

Rank cities by important visitors, high-level encounters, strategic significance, upcoming public events, sectors exposed, and recency.

### 5. Corporate intelligence layer

Expand beyond politics into Fortune 500 CEOs, technology leaders, bank CEOs, private-equity leaders, sovereign wealth fund executives, major investors, philanthropy, health, energy, AI, aerospace, defense, and sports governance.

### 6. Market relevance lens

For every event estimate potential relevance to sectors, countries, and listed companies. This must be framed as potential relevance, never causation.

### 7. Why This Matters engine

Each appearance and encounter should include historical context, previous interactions, strategic relevance, sectors/countries affected, and plausible next steps.

### 8. Signals product

ParleyMap Signals should trigger alerts for new strategic relationships, high-profile meeting announcements, first meetings in years, summit clusters, executive delegations, peace-process movement, and strategic-city concentration.

Users should subscribe by person, country, city, organization, industry, sector, relationship, or topic.

### 9. Source transparency

Every record should show verification level:

- Verified: official or host source confirms the public event.
- Likely: multiple credible public sources support the record.
- Reported: credible media or public reporting exists but official confirmation is missing.

Every card should include source links, source labels, retrieval timestamp, verification explanation, extraction method, and correction/takedown path.

### 10. Institutional product

Target customers include hedge funds, asset managers, family offices, embassies, governments, journalists, consulting firms, intelligence providers, lobbying organizations, corporate strategy teams, and geopolitical risk teams.

Potential tiers:

- Explorer: public map and basic rankings.
- Professional: alerts, saved lists, exports, deeper history.
- Institutional: API, custom watchlists, analyst workspaces, relationship analytics, bulk historical data, and enterprise review tools.

## Tone and visual benchmark

ParleyMap should feel closer to Bloomberg Terminal, FlightRadar24, Palantir, Financial Times, and The Economist than to social media, celebrity tracking, gossip, or surveillance products.

Tone: professional, institutional, data-driven, trustworthy.
