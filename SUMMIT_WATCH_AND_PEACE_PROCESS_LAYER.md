# Source Policy

## Hierarchy

1. Official office, institution, government, royal household, ministry, organization, company, foundation, or event-host source.
2. Public event program, agenda, communique, transcript, official photo caption, or official social account where terms allow.
3. Reputable media or wire reports as secondary corroboration.
4. Discovery tools such as GDELT, Wikidata, OpenSanctions, and search APIs as leads or identity aids, not sole authority for sensitive future location records.

## Future public events

Future engagements need stronger sourcing than past records. At minimum, use an official or host source. Mark all future records as announced, tentative, cancelled, or completed. Default to city-level precision.

## Corrections

If a source changes, the record should show the old source snapshot, new source snapshot, change time, and whether the public card was modified, cancelled, or removed.

## Summary language

Use varied editorial language. Avoid generic filler. State what is known, what is uncertain, and why the public appearance matters. Never imply private meetings or decisions that are not sourced.
