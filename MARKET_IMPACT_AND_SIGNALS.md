# Legal and Editorial Disclaimer

ParleyMap is an editorial and research interface for documented public appearances, public meetings, public speeches, official visits, ceremonies, summits, and officially announced public engagements. It is not a live-location service, security tool, paparazzi product, or endorsement of any person, organization, policy, government, source, map provider, or image provider.

Production deployment requires legal review. This prototype is not legal advice.

## Living-person safeguards

The product must not publish:

- Private residences.
- Hotels or temporary accommodation.
- Hospitals or medical visits unless the person or institution has publicized the information for public-interest reasons and legal review approves it.
- Schools or child-related locations unless already publicized by an official organizer and still safe to publish.
- Leaked itineraries.
- Unofficial sightings.
- Real-time proximity intelligence.
- Inferred routes, convoy paths, flight tracks, or security-sensitive timings.

## Future events

Future public events must be marked as announced, tentative, cancelled, or completed. Default precision is city-level unless the venue is intentionally publicized by the official organizer.

## Corrections

Every production record needs a correction path, source update history, last-checked timestamp, and removal/escalation mechanism.

## v2.0 analytical line disclaimer

ParleyMap may draw homebase-to-destination lines when displaying an individual's public-history map. These are analytical visual aids intended to connect public-source appearances to a public institutional baseline. They are not statements of exact travel route, flight path, convoy route, personal residence, live location, or security movement. They must not be used to infer a person's present whereabouts.

## v2.0 market relevance disclaimer

Market relevance tags identify potentially related sectors, countries, or companies based on public event context. They are not investment advice, trading signals, causation claims, or predictions of financial performance. Confidence labels must be shown and source trails must remain accessible.
